# References: Biological Databases

1. [National Center for Biotechnology Information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Center_for_Biotechnology_Information) - Wikipedia - Overview of NCBI's history, mission, and suite of databases including GenBank, PubMed, and Entrez, which form the backbone of public bioinformatics data access.

2. [UniProt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UniProt) - Wikipedia - Describes the Universal Protein Resource, its component databases Swiss-Prot and TrEMBL, curation processes, and role as the primary protein sequence and annotation repository.

3. [Protein Data Bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein_Data_Bank) - Wikipedia - History and organization of the PDB archive of macromolecular structures, covering deposition, validation, and access to experimentally determined three-dimensional protein structures.

4. Bioinformatics: Sequence and Genome Analysis (2nd Edition) - David Mount - Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory Press - Thorough introduction to biological databases, sequence retrieval methods, and programmatic access tools with practical examples for each major database.

5. Bioinformatics and Functional Genomics (4th Edition) - Jonathan Pevsner - Wiley-Blackwell - Covers NCBI, UniProt, PDB, and KEGG databases with hands-on tutorials showing how to search, retrieve, and analyze biological data programmatically.

6. [NCBI Entrez Help](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK3837/) - NCBI - Official documentation for the Entrez search and retrieval system, explaining cross-database linking, query syntax, and E-utilities for programmatic access.

7. [UniProt Help](https://www.uniprot.org/help) - UniProt Consortium - Comprehensive help pages for searching, downloading, and using the UniProt REST API, including batch retrieval and ID mapping between biological databases.

8. [KEGG: Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes](https://www.kegg.jp/kegg/docs/help.html) - KEGG - Documentation for the KEGG pathway, compound, and disease databases, explaining how metabolic and signaling pathway data is organized and accessed.

9. [Reactome Pathway Database User Guide](https://reactome.org/userguide) - Reactome - Guide to browsing, searching, and analyzing biological pathways in Reactome, including pathway hierarchy navigation, species comparison, and data export options.

10. [RCSB PDB Education Portal](https://www.rcsb.org/education) - RCSB PDB - Educational resources for understanding protein structures in the PDB, including Molecule of the Month articles and guides to structure visualization and interpretation.
