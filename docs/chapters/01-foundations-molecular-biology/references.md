# References: Foundations of Molecular Biology

1. [Central Dogma of Molecular Biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_dogma_of_molecular_biology) - Wikipedia - Explains the flow of genetic information from DNA to RNA to protein, including historical context of Crick's original formulation and modern exceptions.

2. [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of deoxyribonucleic acid structure, replication, and function, covering the double helix, base pairing rules, and biological roles in heredity.

3. [Epigenetics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epigenetics) - Wikipedia - Describes heritable changes in gene expression that do not involve alterations to the DNA sequence, including DNA methylation, histone modification, and chromatin remodeling mechanisms.

4. Molecular Biology of the Cell (7th Edition) - Bruce Alberts - W.W. Norton - The definitive cell biology textbook covering DNA replication, transcription, translation, gene regulation, and mutation with clear diagrams and molecular detail.

5. Molecular Biology: Principles and Practice (3rd Edition) - Michael Cox - W.H. Freeman - Integrates biochemistry and genetics perspectives on molecular biology, with strong coverage of central dogma processes, mutations, and epigenetic mechanisms.

6. [NCBI Molecular Biology Review](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK21054/) - NCBI Bookshelf - Free online chapter from Molecular Biology of the Cell covering DNA, chromosomes, and genome organization with interactive figures and clinical connections.

7. [Khan Academy: Central Dogma](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/biology/gene-expression-central-dogma) - Khan Academy - Video lessons and practice exercises on transcription, translation, and the central dogma, ideal for building foundational understanding before bioinformatics applications.

8. [NHGRI Genetics Glossary](https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary) - National Human Genome Research Institute - Illustrated glossary defining key molecular biology terms including mutation types, epigenetics, and gene expression with accessible explanations.

9. [UniProt Protein Knowledge](https://www.uniprot.org/help/about) - UniProt Consortium - Introduction to the UniProt protein knowledgebase, demonstrating how molecular biology concepts connect to curated protein sequence and function databases.

10. [Nature Education: Essentials of Gene Expression](https://www.nature.com/scitable/topic/gene-expression-and-regulation-15/) - Nature Scitable - Peer-reviewed educational articles on gene expression, RNA processing, and post-translational modification, bridging textbook content with current research.
