## What to Draw

A side-by-side comparison of two cells: a rod-shaped prokaryotic cell on the left half of the image and a rounded eukaryotic animal cell on the right half. Both cells should be drawn at a similar visual scale so they can be compared directly. Leave a clear gap (approximately 10% of image width) between the two cells. Each cell should occupy roughly 40% of the image width, centered vertically.

The prokaryotic cell should appear as a capsule/rod shape (rounded rectangle) viewed in cross-section. The eukaryotic cell should appear as a roughly circular/ovoid shape, also in cross-section, showing internal organelles.

All structures must have distinct, named colors with enough visual area for a 30-40 px circular marker to be placed on them without touching neighboring structures.

**Visual**: A thinner boundary in warm orange (#E67E22), visible as a distinct inner ring separated from the cell wall by a narrow periplasmic gap.

### 3. Nucleoid Region

**Position**: Central area of the prokaryotic cell, slightly left of center within the cell
**Visual**: An irregular tangled mass of fibers in deep violet (#8E44AD), representing the concentrated circular chromosome. No membrane surrounding it — the fibers blend into the cytoplasm at the edges.

### 4. Plasmid

**Position**: Small circular element in the prokaryotic cell, lower-right quadrant of the cell interior, clearly separate from the nucleoid
**Visual**: A small distinct circular ring in bright magenta (#C2185B), about 5-8% of the cell diameter. Should look like a tiny closed loop.

### 5. Prokaryotic Ribosomes

**Position**: Scattered as small dots throughout the prokaryotic cell cytoplasm, concentrated in the upper-right area of the cell interior
**Visual**: Tiny granular dots in teal (#1ABC9C), clustered in groups of 8-12. Each dot is small but visible.

### 6. Flagellum

**Position**: Extending from the right end of the prokaryotic cell, projecting outward to the right
**Visual**: A long wavy/sinusoidal filament in dark teal (#00796B), about 30-40% of the cell's body length. Clearly attached at the cell surface.

### 7. Pili

**Position**: Short hair-like projections from the top surface of the prokaryotic cell
**Visual**: 4-6 short straight filaments in light brown (#8D6E63) extending from the upper surface of the cell. Much shorter and straighter than the flagellum.

### 8. Eukaryotic Plasma Membrane

**Position**: Outer boundary of the right cell, forming the ovoid outline
**Visual**: A smooth boundary line in warm orange (#E67E22) — same color as the prokaryotic plasma membrane to show shared function. Uniform thickness.

### 9. Nucleus

### 10. Nucleolus

**Position**: A smaller dark dense circle inside the nucleus, offset slightly from center
**Visual**: A dark dense sphere in dark indigo (#4A148C), about one-third the diameter of the nucleus. Clearly contained within the nuclear envelope.

### 11. Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum

**Position**: Adjacent to the nucleus on its right side, extending outward in the eukaryotic cell
**Visual**: A network of flattened, parallel folded membranes in medium blue (#3498DB), with small teal dots (#1ABC9C) studded along the membrane surfaces (representing bound ribosomes). Should look like stacked wavy sheets.

### 12. Golgi Apparatus

**Position**: To the lower-right of the nucleus in the eukaryotic cell
**Visual**: A stack of 4-5 flattened curved sacs in golden yellow (#F39C12), crescent-shaped, with small vesicles budding from the edges. Distinct from the ER in color and shape.

### 13. Mitochondrion

**Position**: Lower-left area of the eukaryotic cell, below the nucleus
**Visual**: An elongated oval (bean-shaped) in red-orange (#E74C3C) with an inner folded membrane (cristae) visible as wavy internal lines in a slightly darker red. About 10-12% of cell diameter in length.

### 14. Eukaryotic Ribosomes

**Position**: Scattered as small dots in the eukaryotic cytoplasm, visible in the spaces between organelles, especially upper-right area
**Visual**: Tiny granular dots in teal (#1ABC9C) — same color as prokaryotic ribosomes to show shared function. Scattered freely in the cytoplasm (not on ER).

### 15. Cytoplasm

**Position**: The entire interior space of both cells, visible between organelles
**Visual**: A very light warm cream (#FFF8E1) fill for both cells. In the eukaryotic cell, it fills all space not occupied by organelles. Should be clearly distinct from the white background.
